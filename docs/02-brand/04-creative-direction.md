# 04 — Creative Direction
### Buyue (بيوع) — The Feeling, Emotion & Craft Standard

> **Purpose:** Where Document 03 defines *what* the system is, this document defines *how it should feel*. It is the north star for every subjective decision — the mood, energy, and level of polish that separates an "on-brand" screen from a *premium* one. When a rule doesn't cover a choice, this document decides it.

---

## 1. Brand Feel

**Buyue should feel like a confident, culturally-rooted creative studio that has nothing to prove and everything to deliver.**

Walking through the site should feel like:
- Entering a well-lit, spacious gallery — not a crowded marketplace.
- Meeting an expert who understands you before pitching — calm, precise, warm.
- Watching a beautifully-shot short film — cinematic pacing, intentional reveals.

It should **never** feel like: a template, a generic SaaS site, a loud discount ad, or a cold corporate brochure.

---

## 2. Brand Emotion

The emotional arc we engineer in the visitor:

1. **Intrigue** (hero) — "نبيع كل شيء إلا الكلام" — a confident, slightly witty hook.
2. **Trust** (services + why) — competence and clarity build reassurance.
3. **Belief** (about + values) — the philosophy resonates; this agency *thinks*.
4. **Proof** (clients) — recognizable brands turn belief into certainty.
5. **Desire → Action** (CTA + contact) — "I want this for my brand."

**Core emotion to leave behind:** *confident calm* — the feeling that your brand would be safe, understood, and elevated in Buyue's hands.

---

## 3. Visual Personality

- **Rooted-modern:** Saudi/Gulf warmth expressed through a contemporary geometric system.
- **Editorial:** big type, generous margins, strong image treatment — magazine-grade composition.
- **Bold-but-restrained:** saturated color blocks used decisively, balanced by lots of quiet space.
- **Crafted:** golden-ratio logic, tight alignment, meticulous micro-detail.
- **Cinematic:** motion and imagery create atmosphere and pacing, not decoration.

---

## 4. Mood

| Dimension | Direction |
|-----------|-----------|
| Overall mood | Warm, premium, self-assured, intentional |
| Lighting (imagery) | Natural, golden, cinematic |
| Temperature | Warm-neutral (terracotta/earth) with sage-green calm |
| Density | Spacious, editorial, uncrowded |
| Tone of voice | Confident, direct, human, occasionally witty |
| Pace | Deliberate, unhurried, revealing |

---

## 5. Energy Level

**Medium-high, controlled.** The brand is energetic and confident but never frantic.
- Motion is present on nearly every section, but each movement is smooth and purposeful.
- Color provides the energy; layout provides the calm.
- Think "a strong, steady heartbeat" — not "a flashing strobe."

**Energy dial: 7/10** — enough to feel alive and premium, restrained enough to feel expensive.

---

## 6. Luxury Scale

**8/10 — accessible luxury.** Premium and elevated, but warm and approachable rather than cold/exclusive.
- Luxury comes from **space, craft, motion quality, and photography grade** — not from gold-everything or heavy ornament.
- The terracotta palette keeps it human and Saudi-rooted rather than sterile-luxury.

---

## 7. Modernity Scale

**9/10 — decidedly modern.** Contemporary geometry, clean grids, current motion patterns (smooth scroll, reveals, parallax). Heritage is expressed through *warmth and craft*, never through dated or skeuomorphic styling.

---

## 8. Minimalism Scale

**7/10 — generous minimalism.** Minimal in ornament and clutter; rich in space, type, and color-block impact. Not stark or empty — warm minimalism with confident focal points.

---

## 9. Professionalism Scale

**9/10 — highly professional.** This is a B2B agency courting enterprise and government clients. Every screen must read as trustworthy, precise, and serious — the wit in the copy is the only playful note, and even that is delivered with poise.

---

## 10. Visual Storytelling Direction

The site is a **story told through scroll.** Each page is a chapter; each section is a beat.

- **Home** = the trailer: hook → capability → why → proof → invitation.
- **Services** = the demonstration: the full journey from digital to physical, revealed block by block.
- **About** = the philosophy: vision, mission, values — the "why we exist."
- **Clients** = the evidence: logos and real project imagery.
- **Contact** = the handshake: warm, low-friction, human.

**Storytelling devices:**
- **Progressive reveal** — content enters as the visitor scrolls, pacing the narrative.
- **The angular motif** as a connective thread across chapters (masks, dividers, transitions).
- **Photography as emotion** — human, warm images anchor abstract services in real outcomes.
- **Type as voice** — big confident Arabic headlines carry the brand's spoken personality.

---

## 11. Design Principles

1. **Understand before you decorate.** Structure and clarity first; expression second — mirroring the brand's own promise.
2. **Space is a feature.** Whitespace signals confidence and premium. When in doubt, add space.
3. **One hero per view.** Each screen has a single clear focal point and one primary action.
4. **Color with intent.** Saturated blocks are decisive statements, always balanced by neutral calm.
5. **Motion with meaning.** Every animation clarifies, reveals, or delights — never just moves.
6. **Bilingual equality.** Arabic and English are equals in craft; RTL is designed, not retrofitted.
7. **Consistency is luxury.** The same spacing, motion, and detail everywhere — inconsistency reads as cheap.
8. **Content is sacred.** The approved Arabic copy leads; design serves it, never distorts it.
9. **Prove, don't claim.** Show real work and real logos over adjectives.
10. **Performance is part of the aesthetic.** A premium feel that stutters is not premium.

---

## 12. Things We Want

- Big, confident Arabic headlines with cinematic presence.
- Generous whitespace and editorial rhythm.
- Decisive full-bleed color blocks (Bonfire Flame, Black Powder, Lime Taffy).
- Warm, human, well-graded photography treated with brand overlays/masks.
- Smooth, purposeful motion: reveals, parallax, staggered entrances, refined hovers.
- The angular "carpet-stripe" motif as a recognizable signature.
- Flawless RTL Arabic typography with correct joining and leading.
- A premium, tactile feel on interaction (buttons, cards, cursor).
- Clear hierarchy — the eye always knows where to go next.
- Consistent, systematic detailing across every page.

---

## 13. Things We Never Want

- Template/generic-agency look; anything that could belong to any brand.
- Clutter, cramped layouts, or competing focal points.
- Loud, discount-ad energy or garish color combinations.
- Cheap stock photography or inconsistent image grading.
- Gratuitous animation that slows the page or distracts.
- Broken RTL (mis-mirrored icons, LTR punctuation in Arabic flow, letter-spaced Arabic).
- Low-contrast text (especially small Bonfire-Flame text on light).
- More than one primary CTA competing in a single view.
- Any edit, paraphrase, or "improvement" of the approved Arabic content.
- Dated effects: drop-shadowed logos, gradients-on-everything, skeuomorphism, emoji-as-icons.

---

## 14. Do's

- **Do** lead every page with a confident heading and clear intent.
- **Do** alternate section backgrounds for editorial rhythm.
- **Do** treat all imagery to a consistent warm grade before publishing.
- **Do** use the angular motif to connect sections and mask media.
- **Do** give Arabic ≥1.7 line-height and correct RTL alignment.
- **Do** keep one primary action per view; pair with a subtle secondary.
- **Do** animate entrances with subtle stagger and easing.
- **Do** test every screen in AR (RTL) and EN (LTR) at every breakpoint.
- **Do** respect the performance and accessibility budgets as design constraints.
- **Do** use Quilt Gold as a spark — small, precise highlights.

---

## 15. Don'ts

- **Don't** letter-space Arabic or break letter joining.
- **Don't** put small body text in Bonfire Flame, Quilt Gold, or Lime Taffy on light backgrounds.
- **Don't** stack three saturated color blocks without a neutral between them.
- **Don't** use more than two type families (Zarid + Articulat) — no third font.
- **Don't** animate everything at once; respect pacing and stagger.
- **Don't** distort, recolor, or reconstruct the logo.
- **Don't** crowd the hero — protect its space and single focal point.
- **Don't** ship hover-only interactions without touch equivalents.
- **Don't** let motion run when `prefers-reduced-motion` is set.
- **Don't** invent copy, legal text, or CTAs not in the approved content.

---

## 16. Premium Details

The details that elevate the work from "good" to "premium":

- **Optically-aligned type** — headings and buttons balanced by eye, not just by box.
- **Consistent baseline & spacing rhythm** — everything sits on the 8px grid.
- **Refined easing** — custom cubic-bezier curves, never default `linear`/`ease`.
- **Layered depth** — subtle parallax between background motif, media, and text.
- **Considered empty states** — form success, no-results, and loading states designed, not default.
- **Warm-tinted shadows** — shadows use Black Powder alpha, never pure black.
- **Perfectly graded imagery** — one cohesive color grade across all photos.
- **Micro-timing** — hover, focus, and press states each have their own tuned duration.
- **Edge discipline** — full-bleed panels truly reach the edge; content respects the container.

---

## 17. Micro Details

- Buttons depress slightly on press (scale ~0.98) with tuned easing.
- Links reveal a Bonfire Flame or Quilt Gold underline on hover.
- Cards lift 2–4px with a soft shadow on hover.
- Numbers/statistics count up on scroll-into-view.
- Section dividers use the angular motif, not plain lines.
- Focus rings are branded (Bonfire Flame), not browser-default blue.
- The language switch animates smoothly and mirrors layout without a jarring reflow.
- Cursor subtly acknowledges interactive elements (desktop; see cursor rules in Doc 05).
- Icons share one optical weight and join style.

---

## 18. Visual Consistency Rules

- **One system, everywhere:** identical spacing scale, type scale, radius, shadows, and motion across all five pages.
- **Reusable components:** every recurring block (hero, service card, FAQ, CTA, gallery item) is one component, styled once, reused everywhere.
- **Color roles are fixed:** Bonfire Flame = primary/CTA; Black Powder = ink/dark; Lime Taffy = calm secondary; Quilt Gold = accent spark; Springtime Rain = light surface. Don't reassign roles per page.
- **Motion vocabulary is fixed:** the same reveal, stagger, and hover behaviors repeat site-wide (see Doc 05).
- **Imagery grade is fixed:** one grade, one treatment style.
- **Bilingual parity:** AR and EN versions of a component are visually equivalent in weight and hierarchy.

---

## 19. Cinematic Feel Guidelines

- **Pacing:** reveal content in beats as the visitor scrolls; don't dump everything at once.
- **Depth:** use subtle parallax layers (motif behind, media mid, text front) for a sense of space.
- **Focus pulls:** guide the eye with entrance order and staggered timing, like a camera moving.
- **Atmosphere:** warm imagery + brand overlays create a "scene," not just a background.
- **Transitions:** page and section transitions feel like scene changes — smooth, intentional, never abrupt.
- **Sound-off cinema:** everything reads as premium film even in silence — through composition, grade, and motion.
- **Restraint:** cinematic ≠ busy. The most cinematic moments are often the quietest, most spacious ones.

---

## 20. Luxury Motion Principles

1. **Slow-in, slow-out:** premium motion eases at both ends; nothing starts or stops abruptly.
2. **Weight & physics:** elements feel like they have mass — subtle overshoot/settle, never robotic.
3. **Coordinated, not chaotic:** grouped elements move in orchestrated stagger, not independently.
4. **Subtle over showy:** small, refined movements read as more expensive than large, flashy ones.
5. **Purposeful:** every animation serves reveal, feedback, or continuity.
6. **Consistent timing language:** a shared set of durations/easings across the site (see Doc 05).
7. **Performance-respecting:** transform/opacity only; 60fps or don't ship it.
8. **Reduced-motion aware:** graceful, static fallbacks for users who opt out.

---

## 21. Premium UI Characteristics

- **Generous, consistent spacing** — the single biggest premium signal.
- **Confident typography** — large, well-set headings; comfortable body reading.
- **Decisive color** — used sparingly and with intent.
- **Tactile interactions** — every interactive element responds with tuned motion.
- **Impeccable alignment** — nothing is a pixel off; optical balance respected.
- **Cohesive imagery** — one grade, one mood.
- **Considered states** — hover, focus, active, disabled, loading, empty, error, success all designed.
- **Fast & smooth** — instant response, buttery scroll, no layout shift.
- **Flawless bilingual** — Arabic and English both feel native, never translated-as-afterthought.
- **Quiet confidence** — the UI never shouts; it lets the work and space speak.

---

## 22. Creative Guardrails Summary

| If unsure about… | Default to… |
|------------------|-------------|
| Spacing | more space |
| Color | less saturation, one accent |
| Motion | subtler, slower ease |
| Type size | bigger heading, comfortable body |
| Ornament | remove it |
| Number of CTAs per view | one primary |
| Photography | warm, human, consistently graded |
| A new pattern | reuse an existing component |

**The single test for any creative decision:** *Does this make Buyue feel more rooted, more modern, and more confident — and does it prove that "we sell everything but talk"?* If not, cut it.
