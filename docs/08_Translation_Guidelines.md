# 08 — Translation Guidelines
### Buyue (بيوع) — Arabic → English Localization Standard

> **Purpose:** Define how English content is produced for the site. English is **not** independent copy — it is a premium marketing translation *derived from the approved Arabic*. This document ensures the English version carries the same meaning, emotion, persuasion, hierarchy, and brand voice as the Arabic, without ever being literal or robotic.

---

## 1. The Golden Rules (non-negotiable)

1. **Arabic is always the source language.** Every English string is translated **from the approved Arabic in Document 02** — never from an idea, a brief, or a prior English draft.
2. **English is translated ONLY from Arabic.** Do not write English first and back-fill Arabic. Do not localize English from a third language.
3. **Never translate literally / word-for-word.** Translate *meaning and intent*, not tokens. A grammatically-correct literal rendering that loses the punch is a failed translation.
4. **Translate using premium marketing language.** The English must read as if written by a senior copywriter for a high-end agency — confident, polished, natural.
5. **Maintain emotion.** The feeling of the Arabic (confidence, warmth, wit) must survive into English.
6. **Maintain persuasion.** Persuasive force and CTA pull must be equal or better in English.
7. **Maintain hierarchy.** Headings stay headings, subheads stay subheads, body stays body — same structural weight.
8. **Maintain CTA intent.** A CTA's *action and urgency* are preserved, even if the literal words change.
9. **Maintain formatting.** Lists stay lists, bold stays bold, line breaks and section order are preserved.
10. **Maintain section structure.** The English page has the exact same sections, in the same order, as the Arabic.
11. **Maintain brand voice.** Rooted-modern, confident, human, occasionally witty — consistent across both languages.

> **The Arabic remains sacred and verbatim (Document 02). This document governs the English only.** Producing the English never permits editing the Arabic.

---

## 2. Brand Voice in English

The English voice must sound like Buyue — the same personality expressed in Doc 04:

- **Confident, not boastful** — "We build brand presence that doesn't just compete — it commands the market," not "We are the best agency ever."
- **Clear, not clever-for-its-own-sake** — direct sentences; wit is deployed with poise (as in the hero pun).
- **Human and warm** — speaks *to* a decision-maker, not *at* them.
- **Rooted-modern** — professional and contemporary; avoid both stiff corporate jargon and casual slang.
- **Impact-led** — echoes "measured by impact, not post count."

**Register:** professional B2B, addressed to marketing managers, founders, and government/enterprise buyers. Prefer active voice, strong verbs, and concrete nouns.

---

## 3. Translation Method (process)

For each Arabic string:

1. **Read for meaning + emotion.** What is it *doing* (informing, persuading, inviting)? What does it *feel* like?
2. **Identify the function** (heading / subhead / body / CTA / label / FAQ) and preserve that function's constraints (length, tone, weight).
3. **Draft the English** to reproduce meaning, emotion, and persuasion — *not* the word order.
4. **Refine as marketing copy** — tighten, strengthen verbs, ensure it flows naturally to a native English reader.
5. **Check parity** — same hierarchy, same section, same formatting, same CTA intent.
6. **Native review** — an English-native marketing reviewer confirms it reads as premium original copy, and a bilingual reviewer confirms fidelity to the Arabic meaning.

---

## 4. What to Preserve vs. What May Change

| Preserve (must not change) | May adapt (should change for quality) |
|----------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| Meaning & core message | Word order & sentence structure |
| Emotion & tone | Idioms (use the English equivalent, not a literal one) |
| Persuasive intent | Phrasing/length (within the function's limits) |
| CTA action & urgency | Metaphors (localize to resonate in English) |
| Hierarchy (H/subhead/body) | Punctuation style (follow English norms) |
| Section order & structure | Grammar (follow English grammar, not Arabic) |
| Formatting (lists/bold/breaks) | Connectors and rhythm |
| Brand voice | — |
| Proper nouns / brand names (Buyue, stc, etc.) | — |

## 5. Handling Specific Elements

- **Brand & product names:** keep as-is — Buyue, Noble Business Group, stc, Zain, ASUS, SNB, Isuzu, NAPCO, MAKKA, Jazeera Paints, Vodafone, Coca-Cola, Red Bull, Talabat, Foodics, BAVO, Sparx, Al Ahly SC.
- **Technical terms:** SEO, UGC, EGC, FGC, Outdoor — keep as recognized English terms (they appear in Arabic too).
- **Places:** Khobar / Al-Khobar, Al-Thuqbah, Dhahran Road, Saudi Arabia, Egypt — use standard English spellings; confirm preferred romanization with client.
- **Numbers/stats:** keep the figure ("more than 30 brands"); use Western numerals in English.
- **Addresses, phones, email:** reproduce exactly; do not translate. (Note the unresolved email/domain discrepancy — Doc 01 Constraints — must be confirmed, not localized.)
- **CTAs:** translate the *intent*, keep them short, punchy, and action-first (see below).
- **FAQ questions:** keep them conversational and scannable, matching the Arabic's approachable question style.

## 7. Good vs. Bad Translations (worked examples)

> These use real Buyue content. "Bad" = literal/robotic/off-voice. "Good" = premium marketing translation faithful to meaning, emotion, and persuasion.

### Example A — Hero headline
- **Arabic:** «نبيع كل شيء إلا الكلام»
- ❌ **Bad (literal):** "We sell everything except words." — technically close, but flat; loses the confident pun.
- ⚠ **Weak:** "We sell everything but talk." — better, but reads slightly ambiguous.
- ✅ **Good:** **"We Sell Everything — Except Talk."** — keeps the wit and defiance; the dash adds the confident beat. *(Confirm final wording with client; the pun on "sales/بيوع" is the point — protect it.)*
- **Why:** preserves emotion (confident wit) and intent (substance over words), reads as a crafted tagline.

### Example B — Hero body
- **Arabic:** بيوع وكالة تسويق وإعلان متكاملة، نساعد العلامات التجارية على بناء حضور قوي في السوق من خلال استراتيجيات واضحة، محتوى مؤثر، حملات ذكية، وتنفيذ إبداعي يربط الفكرة بالنتيجة.
- ❌ **Bad (literal):** "Buyue is an integrated marketing and advertising agency, we help the commercial brands on building a strong presence in the market through clear strategies, effective content, smart campaigns, and creative execution that links the idea with the result."
- ✅ **Good:** **"Buyue is a full-service marketing and advertising agency. We help brands build a powerful market presence through clear strategy, content that resonates, smart campaigns, and creative execution that connects the idea to the result."**
- **Why:** natural English rhythm, stronger verbs ("resonates"), removes literal artifacts ("commercial brands", "on building"), keeps every idea and the persuasive build.

### Example C — Differentiator (heading + body)
- **Arabic:** نركز على النتائج — نؤمن أن التسويق الحقيقي يُقاس بالأثر، لا بعدد المنشورات.
- ❌ **Bad (literal):** "We focus on the results — We believe that the real marketing is measured by the impact, not by the number of the publications."
- ✅ **Good:** **"We Focus on Results — Real marketing is measured by impact, not by post count."**
- **Why:** tighter, punchier, keeps the exact contrast (impact vs. volume) and the confident tone; drops literal articles that read as non-native.

### Example D — About, philosophical line
- **Arabic:** لنخلق حضوراً تجارياً لا يكتفي بالمنافسة، بل يفرض نفسه في السوق.
- ❌ **Bad (literal):** "To create a commercial presence that is not satisfied with the competition, but imposes itself in the market."
- ✅ **Good:** **"To create a brand presence that doesn't just compete — it commands the market."**
- **Why:** "commands the market" carries the force of "يفرض نفسه" in idiomatic English; keeps the escalation and confidence intact.

### Example E — Service value line
- **Arabic:** نمنح علامتك شكلاً وصوتاً واضحين يساعدانها على الظهور بثقة في السوق.
- ❌ **Bad (literal):** "We give your brand a clear shape and voice that help it to appear with confidence in the market."
- ✅ **Good:** **"We give your brand a clear look and voice — so it shows up in the market with confidence."**
- **Why:** "look and voice" is the natural English pairing; the em-dash preserves the cause→effect beat; warm and confident.

### Example F — CTA (colloquial warmth)
- **Arabic:** عايز نشتغل سوا على مشروعك الجاي؟ احجز استشارة
- ❌ **Bad (literal):** "You want that we work together on your coming project? Reserve a consultation."
- ✅ **Good:** **"Want to work together on your next project? Book a consultation."**
- **Why:** keeps the friendly, direct invitation; natural CTA verb ("Book"); no dialect artifacts.

### Example G — FAQ
- **Arabic (Q):** في أي دول تعمل بيوع؟ — **(A):** تعمل بيوع بشكل أساسي في المملكة العربية السعودية ومصر، وتقدم خدماتها لعلامات تجارية محلية وإقليمية وعالمية.
- ❌ **Bad (literal):** "In which countries does Buyue work? — Buyue works basically in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia and Egypt, and it presents its services to local, regional and international commercial brands."
- ✅ **Good:** **"Which countries does Buyue operate in? — Buyue works primarily across Saudi Arabia and Egypt, serving local, regional, and global brands."**
- **Why:** natural question phrasing, "operate in", cleaner list, removes literal padding ("basically", "presents its services", "commercial").

## 8. Common Literal-Translation Traps (avoid these)

| Arabic pattern | Literal trap | Do instead |
|----------------|--------------|-----------|
| علامة تجارية | "commercial brand" | **brand** |
| نساعد ... على | "help ... on" | **help ... [verb]** (help brands build) |
| يقدم خدماته | "presents its services" | **offers / provides services** |
| بشكل أساسي | "basically" | **primarily / mainly** |
| من خلال | "through the way of" | **through / via** |
| الحضور | "the presence" (overusing "the") | drop unnecessary articles |
| يربط الفكرة بالنتيجة | "links the idea with the result" | **connects idea to outcome** |
| القوالب الجاهزة | "the ready templates" | **off-the-shelf / cookie-cutter templates** |
| Overusing "the" | Arabic definiteness → literal "the" everywhere | use natural English article usage |

**Rules of thumb:**
- Drop the reflexive over-use of "the".
- Replace "commercial brand" with "brand".
- Turn noun-heavy Arabic into verb-led English.
- Localize idioms/metaphors to their English equivalents.
- Keep sentences shorter than a literal render usually produces.

## 9. Formatting & Structural Parity

- English page = same sections, same order, same headings/subheads as Arabic (Document 02).
- Preserve lists as lists, bold as bold, and the "ماذا نقدم؟ / القيمة (What we offer / The value)" structure in service blocks.
- Match line breaks in headings where they carry emphasis.
- Titles/meta: translate the SEO title/description faithfully and idiomatically; keep the keyword *intent* — but English keywords should read naturally, not be a literal keyword dump.
- English is **LTR**; ensure the layout mirrors correctly and English text is never forced into RTL flow.

---

## 10. SEO in English

- Translate each page's title/description to premium, natural English that still targets the same search intent (marketing agency, Saudi/Khobar, Egypt, branding).
- Do **not** literal-translate the Arabic keyword list — research the equivalent English search terms and phrase them naturally.
- Keep brand + place names consistent across both locales.
- Provide `hreflang` alternates so AR and EN versions are correctly associated (Doc 07 §7).

---

## 11. Quality Bar & Review

An English string passes only when:
- [ ] It reads as **premium, native English marketing copy** (not translated-sounding).
- [ ] It faithfully carries the Arabic **meaning, emotion, and persuasion**.
- [ ] Its **function, hierarchy, and formatting** match the Arabic exactly.
- [ ] Its **CTA intent** (action + urgency) is preserved.
- [ ] It uses the **Buyue brand voice** consistently.
- [ ] Brand/technical/place names are correct and consistent.
- [ ] It was **derived from the Arabic**, reviewed by both a native-English marketing reviewer and a bilingual fidelity reviewer.

> If any check fails, revise the English — **never** the Arabic.

---

## 12. Summary

- **Arabic = source, sacred, verbatim.** English = premium translation of it.
- **Meaning over words. Emotion over grammar-literalism. Persuasion over precision-for-its-own-sake.**
- Preserve hierarchy, structure, formatting, CTA intent, and brand voice.
- When Arabic and English disagree, the Arabic is right and the English is rewritten to match its intent.
